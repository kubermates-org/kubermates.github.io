  Applications Virtualization Share At Bradesco, one of the largest financial institutions
  in Brazil and Latin America, the ability to scale is crucial. Automation plays a
  central role in this journey, and Red Hat Ansible Automation Platform has become
  the foundation supporting thousands of jobs executed daily across mission-critical
  environments. As automation expanded across teams, systems, and domains, Bradesco
  reached a new stage of maturity. Execution at scale was already well established,
  delivering efficiency and speed. However, operating automation at this level within
  a highly regulated financial environment introduced a new challenge: Enabling governance,
  visibility, and control aligned with internal audit, compliance, and risk management
  standards. Execution remained robust, but the growing scale of automation required
  a broader view, where observability and governance became as important as execution
  itself. Gaining comprehensive oversight at Bradesco’s scale required a strategic
  approach to observability. Large financial institutions have extensive governance
  expectations and they needed execution metrics to fit their needs. Automation data
  needed to be contextualized and connected to enterprise workflows, including IT
  Service Management (ITSM) and auditing processes. To address these requirements,
  the Bradesco automation team uses the open architecture and robust APIs (metrics)
  of Red Hat Ansible Automation Platform as the foundation for a governance-focused
  observability strategy. By building on top of the platform’s execution data, the
  team was able to transform raw automation information into actionable insights that
  support operational decision-making, compliance, and long-term sustainability. Not
  only did Red Hat Ansible Automation Platform help teams identify obsolete assets,
  enforce accountability, and reduce operational and compliance risk in large-scale
  environments, but the consolidated view helps teams assess overall automation health
  and trends over time.'
