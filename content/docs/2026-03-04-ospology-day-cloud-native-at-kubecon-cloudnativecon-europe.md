summary: 'Peer mentoring and group discussions on cloud strategy management Topics
  to dig into Join us! Posted on March 4, 2026 by OSPO Ana Jimenez Santamaria, Program
  Manager at the TODO Group Cloud native management has matured quickly. Platform
  engineering is now a cross-organization product, supply chain security expectations
  continue to rise, and regulation has moved from a future concern to a present-day
  roadmap constraint. As a result, many organizations are recognizing that they can’t
  sustainably adopt Kubernetes and other CNCF projects by “consuming” them alone.
  Long-term success increasingly depends on having an intentional approach to upstream
  contribution, governance, compliance, and community health OSPOlogy Day Cloud Native
  is an in-person OSPOlogy Day edition hosted by CNCF in collaboration with the TODO
  Europe Chapter, designed to help open source managers navigate strategy and operations
  specifically in cloud native ecosystems. It creates space for the conversations
  with structure, mentors, and peers who live these every day problems for open source
  leaders involved in open source management, engaging with these areas of organizations
  to streamline their cloud strategy and operations The format is composed of lightning
  intros with small-group roundtables where participants tackle challenges, share
  patterns, and demo tooling, all under the Chatham House Rule to encourage candid
  discussion. The early submissions and suggested discussion themes reflect the kinds
  of questions OSPOs and open source management teams are navigating right now in
  cloud native environments: Open source education that matches reality : bridging
  “how to open a PR” with the social/political dynamics newcomers actually experience.
  Project lifecycle and decline : recognizing early signals, responding responsibly,
  and treating sunsets as a governance phase (not a failure). CNCF project growth
  and sustainability : what support is most useful post-acceptance, and how end-user
  orgs drive adoption beyond “certification fatigue. ” Compliance, security and governance
  : operationalizing license policy, verification pipelines, and decision-making with
  better data. Local communities inside global ecosystems : why local groups thrive
  and when local focus can fragment impact. Sovereignty and regulation : EU CRA implications
  and sovereign cloud approaches aligned with the CNCF landscape. OSPOlogy Day Cloud
  Native will take place on March 23, 2026, from 13:00 to 16:00 CET at RAI Amsterdam.'
