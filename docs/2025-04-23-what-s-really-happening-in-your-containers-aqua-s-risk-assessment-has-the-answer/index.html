📝 Summary

What’s Really Happening in Your Containers? Aqua’s Risk Assessment Has the Answer Beyond the Static: Why Runtime Context Matters Cutting Through the Noise What You’ll Gain from CSRA Built on a Decade of Threat Research A Smarter First Step Get Started Today Containers may be mainstream, but securing them in production remains a moving target. As AI adoption scales and environments grow more complex, so too do the risks, especially at runtime, where traditional tools struggle to provide meaningful visibility. These are not legacy exploits like port scans or brute force attempts. Attackers are targeting what happens inside your environment, at runtime, where misconfigurations, unexpected behaviors, and subtle anomalies can quietly introduce business risk. Yet most security teams are flying blind in production. They have tools that check for vulnerabilities before deployment, scan code for secrets, or assess infrastructure misconfigurations. What they don’t have is visibility into what’s actually happening right now, in the workloads already running in their environment. That’s the gap Aqua is closing with the launch of the Container Security Risk Assessment (CSRA). Security tooling has largely focused on pre-production controls like image scanning, CI/CD pipeline checks, and IaC validation. It is logical, and even easier than runtime. But once containers are running, things change. Configurations drift.
